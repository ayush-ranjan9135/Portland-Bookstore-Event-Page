The Evening

A Gathering of
Literary Voices

Every few months, we close the shelves early and open the floor to the writers who fill them. This August, four extraordinary authors come together to share new work, read from their pages, and talk about the craft of turning lived experience into language.

Whether you're a lifelong devotee of literary fiction or just beginning to find your reading life, this evening is designed to feel like the best dinner-party conversation you've ever been part of — with books as the starting point.

Featured Authors

The Voices of
the Evening

Four distinct worlds, one transformative night at Portland's most intimate literary venue.

E.M
Literary Fiction

Elena Marchetti

The Cartographer's Daughter

Elena's debut novel traces three generations of a Venetian family through hand-drawn maps and the silences between them. Winner of the PEN/Faulkner Award and hailed by the Times as "a masterwork of quiet devastation." She lives and writes in Astoria, Oregon.

Website Instagram
J.O
Poetry

James Okafor

Songs from the Root

James's debut collection moves between Lagos and Portland, finding the spiritual geometry connecting both cities. His poems have appeared in The Paris Review and were shortlisted for the National Book Award. He teaches at Reed College.

Website Instagram
S.V
Short Stories

Sarah Voss

Everything Between

Sarah's collection captures the overlooked spaces of American life — laundromats, hospital waiting rooms, rest stops — with precision that turns the ordinary luminous. A Pushcart Prize recipient and former Stegner Fellow at Stanford University.

Website Instagram
D.P
Memoir

David Park

The Garden of Small Things

David's memoir follows his return to his grandmother's garden in rural Oregon after years in Seoul, weaving together botany, grief, and the inheritance of place. Critics have called his writing "Thoreau for a fractured century." He grew up in Hillsboro.
